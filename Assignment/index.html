Global Energy Consumption

How is Our World Powering the Future?

This interactive visualization offers a comprehensive look into the evolution of global energy consumption over the past century. It explores the increasing demand driven by industrialization, population growth, and technological advancements, while also highlighting the shift from traditional fuels like coal and oil to cleaner, renewable sources such as wind, solar, and hydropower. Hover over the charts and graphs to dive deeper into the data, uncover regional trends, and explore the driving forces behind the global energy transition.

Global Energy Demand: Insights into Primary Consumption

An essential indicator of a country's energy demand is its total consumption, measured in terawatt-hours (TWh). From 1985 to 2021, global primary energy consumption has shifted dramatically, reflecting changes in industrial growth, population dynamics, and the adoption of diverse energy sources.



Source: Primary Energy Consumption by Source and Population by Our World in Data

Explaining Global Energy Substitution Over Time

Global energy substitution is the process in which various energy sources have been replaced over time, influenced by advancements in technology, availability of resources, and evolving societal demands

Key Insights

  • Traditional Biomass Dominance (Pre-1900): In the early years, traditional biomass, such as wood, was the dominant energy source. Coal began to gain traction slowly during the late 1800s.
  • Industrialization Era (1900-1960): The use of coal grew significantly during the early 20th century as industrialization spread globally, with oil and gas joining the mix by mid-century.
  • Modern Energy Mix (Post-1960): Post-1960 saw rapid growth in oil and gas usage, while nuclear energy emerged in the 1970s. In recent decades, renewable sources like wind, solar, and biofuels have seen substantial growth.


Source: Global primary energy consumption by source by Our World in Data

Tracking Renewable Energy Consumption Trends Globally

From 1980 to 2023, the ranking of countries around the world in terms of renewable energy consumption has fluctuated, reflecting global shifts in energy policies, resource management, and technological advancements. By examining how different nations rise and fall in these rankings, we gain valuable insights into their evolving commitment to renewable energy and how their strategies compare on a global scale, highlighting key trends in the transition to more sustainable energy sources.



Source: Energy Consumption by source by Our World in Data

Explaining CO2 Emissions Over Time

Explaining CO₂ emissions over time is essential for understanding how global energy consumption patterns have evolved and how they relate to climate change. CO₂ emissions are primarily the result of burning fossil fuels—such as coal, oil, and natural gas—for energy production, transportation, and industrial activities.
