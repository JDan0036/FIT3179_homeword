Global Energy Consumption

Energy consumption is a critical factor in the development and sustainability of modern economies. Over the past century, global energy demand has risen significantly, driven by industrialization, population growth, and advancements in technology. The sources of energy have also evolved, with a gradual transition from traditional fuels like coal and oil towards renewable and cleaner sources such as wind, solar, and hydropower.

Explaining the Global Primary Energy Consumption for 2021

This visualization highlights global energy consumption in 2021, measured in terawatt-hours (TWh). Countries are color-coded, with darker shades of purple representing higher energy usage and lighter shades indicating lower consumption.

Key Insights

  • High Energy Consumers: Countries like the United States, China, and Russia show the highest energy consumption, driven by industrial activity and large populations.
  • Moderate Consumption: Nations in Europe, such as Germany and the UK, consume significant energy but not as much as the top global consumers.
  • Low Energy Consumers: Many African, Southeast Asian, and South American countries have much lower energy consumption due to smaller populations and less industrial activity.

Explaining Global Energy Substitution Over Time

This area chart visualizes the global shift in energy sources from the 19th century to 2023. It highlights the growth and substitution of various energy types over time, measured in terawatt-hours (TWh). By clicking on the legend, you can filter specific energy sources to focus on the ones you're most interested in.

Key Insights

  • Traditional Biomass Dominance (Pre-1900): In the early years, traditional biomass, such as wood, was the dominant energy source. Coal began to gain traction slowly during the late 1800s.
  • Industrialization Era (1900-1960): The use of coal grew significantly during the early 20th century as industrialization spread globally, with oil and gas joining the mix by mid-century.
  • Modern Energy Mix (Post-1960): Post-1960 saw rapid growth in oil and gas usage, while nuclear energy emerged in the 1970s. In recent decades, renewable sources like wind, solar, and biofuels have seen substantial growth.

Explaining Renewable Energy Ranking Over Time

This bump chart provides a clear view of how different North American countries rank in terms of their hydropower energy consumption over time, from 1980 to 2023. It highlights the changes and stability in the relative consumption of hydropower among these countries, emphasizing their positions rather than their exact consumption values.
